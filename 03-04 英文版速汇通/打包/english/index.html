Dear Customer:

On 24 April 2022 you will no longer be able to transfer or receive funds from Citibank Australia accounts through Citibank Global Transfer.

You can still use Citibank Online (www.citibank.com.cn) to transfer funds to Citibank Australia accounts using Citibank Australia’s SWIFT code - CITIAU2X. Do note that existing Australia payees in Citibank Global Transfer will be removed and you will have to create payees for SWIFT transfer in Citibank Online.Your Citibank Global Transfer service to all other countries will continue to work as usual.

To help you prepare for this change we’ve created a quick tutorial and answers to frequently asked questions.

Yours sincerely,

The Citi Team

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I transfer money overseas using SWIFT? +
Pre 24 April 2022, you will need to set up a payee through the ‘Another Overseas Bank Account’ route (highlighted below) on Citibank Online. This will require you to enter in the payee’s details, including address and bank account details. Bank account details can include a SWIFT code, International Bank Account Number (IBAN) or another country specific code. If you are unsure, please check with the payee who will be able to confirm with their bank what routing code to use. Please also note currently the daily limit for SWIFT transfer through Citi Online is USD 20,000 or equivalent foreign currencies.
What details do I provide if someone’s transferring money to me from Australia CITIGROUP PTY LIMITED? +
To receive an international funds transfer, customers will need to provide their account number and Citibank China’s SWIFT code (CITICNSX) to the payer.
Are there any fees or charges from Citi to implement this change? +
A fee waiver will be offered to customer who transfer fund to Australia CITIGROUP PTY LIMITED.

Should you have any enquiry, please contact Citibank China service hotline: 95038.
Will my existing CGT payee details in
Citibank Online be removed from 24
April 2022 onwards? +
Existing Australian CGT payee details will be removed in Citibank Online from 24 April 2022 onwards. Before then, please be reminded to use the account details you previously used to create CGT payees to create your payees for SWIFT transfer in Citibank Online, together with the corresponding SWIFT code below:

Country/Region Bank Name Corresponding SWIFT Code BSB code
AUSTRALIA CITIGROUP PTY LIMITED CITIAU2X NA
Will the SWIFT transaction take longer if made on a weekend, a public/bank holiday, or after the cut-off time? +
SWIFT transactions usually take up to 72 hours to process. There may be circumstances where this could take longer, such as if public holidays and weekends occur side by side.
